We really liked the classic craftsman look of the house. The symmetry and proportions were very appealing. The detail level on windows and the porch railing are simple, but look very good. I'm happy with the way the paint job came out - the battleship gray with the pure white trim to pop it out.

The rocking chair porch is a big plus here. The house sits high above the road and has views. I really like outdoor space to sit and watch the world go by.

I like hard wood floors. The carpet runner up the stairs is good for maintenance, safety. The house has a great woodwork partition to the stairs. I really like wood accents and trim without going to "mountain cabin" excessive.

The leaded glass would be a bit much for Coyle, but I do like windows with some sort of divisions - not just giant picture windows.

On the south, the living room has stained glass windows framing a wood burning stove. These windows are getting close to over-the-top even for this house, but I like the symmetry around the fireplace. Note the detail level on the windows - simple and classic

The spacious, formal dining room features traditional built-ins and flows comfortably from the living room. The book shelves and hutch really fit this house (although I don't think the hutch is original). Nothing so formal for Coyle, but still, woodwork accents are great.

The first floor offers a quiet, relaxed office or spare bedroom with adjacent half-bath. There are quite a few large, double hung windows in these old houses. The traditional single, overhead light fixture is a big problem though. 100 year old lighting schemes are crap, but I'd hate to retrofit an old house. For Coyle, of course, much better, modern lighting

The wainscot with a chair rail and a wall or pedestal sink is a look we've used at both Latona and Thackeray for bathrooms. In Latona, the bathroom floors are vinyl, in Thackeray, tile. I prefer the tile, but I'm open. (I don't like the paint color picked for the bathrooms at Latona.)

The custom oak cabinetry is really well made (all custom), but its looks so 80s (which it is). I'm also not crazy about the way the cabinets don't go to ceiling height, although there is crown molding which helps. We updated appliances, but nothing else in the kitchen. I think the flooring is really ugly, especially with the blue paint (seriously 1980s), but this is where "its a rental" kicked in. Vinyl or wood is great for the kitchen, not sure if the expense of stone would be worth it for a small kitchen at Coyle

The breakfast nook is well lit with all the updated windows. Again, I like the windows divided like that. The wainscot to the ceiling seems a little unusual, but I like it. Still that ugly flooring...

We rebuilt the cedar deck. In the past, I've preferred cedar over Trex or other engineered material, but I think I can now be convinced for Trex. They seem to be looking better these days, and the lower maintenance at Coyle would be a requirement.

The east facing bedroom offers panaramic views of the Cascades. The large, original, double hungs make it very nice. I'm not crazy about the paint colors we picked on any of the bedrooms.

The large, south facing, master bedroom offers plenty of space. The previous owner knocked out a wall to a closet to expand this beyond the original small bedroom.

This west facing bedroom offers huge casements that flood the room with light in the evenings, provide great territorial views, and even a peek-a-boo view of the Space Needle! (Unfortunately, they don't operate, so this room bakes in the summer.) These windows are another example of the interior wood trim level I like

We gutted this bathroom, added some space from an adjacent closet. I like the vinyl floor we picked. I also like solid-surface tub/shower surrounds (not one-pieces!) Again, here, we have wainscot and a pedestal sink. Again, the paint color I don't like. The window came that way. Not sure if I like it, but wasn't willing to change it either.

Over 500 sq. feet of storage in the dry, high ceiling basement
